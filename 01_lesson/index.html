  1. Grand Canyon National Park, Arizona, US

    2. Grand Canyon National Park, located in Arizona, is renowned for its immense and visually stunning canyon carved by the Colorado River.
    The park spans over 1.2 million acres, offering breathtaking vistas, diverse wildlife, and numerous recreational opportunities such as hiking, rafting, and sightseeing.
    Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Grand Canyon attracts millions of visitors each year, making it one of the most popular natural landmarks in the United States.

    Grand Canyon National Park 20 South Entrance Road Grand Canyon, AZ 86023 USA

  2. The famous Mount Fuji

    3. You can’t visit Japan without seeing it, even if it is from the plane window or squinting into the distance at the Tokyo Met Observation Tower.
    Aside from being a famous landmark and symbol of the country, Mount Fuji is a natural wonder in its own right.
    Whether you choose to see it from across the lake in Hakone, or from Lake Kawaguchi, you can even climb a different mountain to see it from a height. There’s nothing more stunning than a sunrise at the peak, and nothing as hard-earned either!

    Mount Fuji, Kitayama, Fujinomiya, Shizuoka 418-0112, Japan.

  3. Amanohashidate’s bridge between heaven and earth

    4. Considered one of Japan’s top three views, the Amanohashidate sandbar in Miyazu Bay is Kyoto’s favorite summer getaway destination.
    The sandbar is said to either look like a bridge between heaven and earth (as the name alludes to) or a dragon – depending on who you talk to.
    You can walk or bike along the sandbar, or for the best views head to Amanohashidate View Land or take the cable car up to Kasamatsu Park.
    Plus, there are plenty of other things to do in the area, including taking cruises around the bay.

    Below is a description list of notable features and attractions associated with Amanohashidate:

    Amanohashidate Sandbar
    • Length and Shape:

      • The sandbar stretches approximately 3.6 kilometers (2.2 miles) and is covered with around 8,000 pine trees.

    • Walking and Cycling Path:

      • Visitors can walk or rent bicycles to traverse the sandbar, enjoying the scenic beauty along the way.

    Chionji Temple
    • Historical Significance:

      • A Buddhist temple situated near the southern entrance of the sandbar, known for its beautiful architecture and serene environment.

    • Monju Bosatsu:

      • The temple is dedicated to Monju Bosatsu, the Bodhisattva of Wisdom, attracting visitors seeking academic success.

  4. FAIRY MEADOWS

    5. Fairy Meadows, located in Pakistan's Gilgit-Baltistan region, is a stunning grassland area at the base of Nanga Parbat, the world's ninth-highest mountain.
    Known for its breathtaking views and serene environment, it offers a picturesque camping spot for adventurers and nature enthusiasts.
    Accessible by a challenging jeep ride followed by a trek, Fairy Meadows is celebrated for its lush green meadows and panoramic vistas.

    Diamer District, Gilgit-Baltistan, Pakistan
